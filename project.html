  1. Image Macros: These are memes that combine an image with overlaid text, typically in bold, white font. The text often conveys a humorous or relatable message.

  2. Reaction GIFs: These are short animated clips or images taken from movies, TV shows, or other sources that depict a particular reaction or emotion. They are often used to express a feeling or response in an online conversation.

  3. Exploitable Memes: These are memes that consist of a template or format that can be easily customized or manipulated. People can add their own text or images to create new variations and jokes.

  4. Dank Memes: This term refers to memes that are considered to be unconventional, absurd, or edgy. They often involve dark humor, surrealism, or nonsensical elements.

  5. Wholesome Memes: These are memes that promote positive and uplifting messages, often centered around kindness, compassion, or wholesome content.

  6. Relatable Memes: These memes focus on everyday experiences or situations that many people can relate to. They often involve humorous observations about life, relationships, work, or pop culture.

  7. Internet Challenges: These are meme trends that involve people participating in specific activities or tasks and sharing their experiences online. Examples include the Ice Bucket Challenge or the Harlem Shake.

  8. Video Memes: These are memes in the form of short videos, often shared on platforms like TikTok or Vine. They can involve skits, parodies, remixes, or humorous clips.
